The C3-PRO Components

C3-PRO Research Kit App

Installed on the iPhone, the App is built on the top of Apple's Research Kit. It manages the consent workflow for patients willing to enroll to a study, and drive surveys and activity reports performed by the patients. Data is captured as FHIR resources and encrypted in-device using AES and a Public RSA Key generated from an in-house server. The used FHIR resources in the first release are:

  • QuestionnaireAnswers: It codifies activity reports and the answers of the surveys.
  • Contract: It captures the information regarding the consent form.
  • Observation: It includes information extracted from Apple's Health Kit.
  • Patient: It only includes information regarding the US state of the patient.
  • Questionnaire: The app consumes this resource from the C3-PRO-SERVER.

C3-PRO Server

The current release runs in a highly reliable AWS environment to ensure highly availability and scalability for the App. It's in charge of

  • Handeling the REST calls from the App and temporally store the encrypted resources.
  • Delivering static content like Questionnaire resources.
  • Managing OAuth 2 two-legged protocol to authorize App requests.

C3-PRO Consumer

It's a daemon that runs in-house under very restrictive access. It consumes from the SQS, decrypts the FHIR resources and sends them into i2b2 using the newly created C3-PRO FHIR I2B2 Cell. To ensure that all data is recorded properly, encrypted raw data is also stored in-house as BLOB.

C3-PRO FHIR I2B2 cell

It's an i2b2 FHIR DSTU-2 compliant cell. In this first release, it accepts PUT and POST rest calls of the resources mentioned above.
